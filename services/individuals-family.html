  • Salary and job role compliance check for renewal

  • Coordinating on your behalf any request for further documentation by SIRI

Why It's Priced Lower

Renewals follow the same SIRI process as a first application, but without the biometric overseas complexities that often arise for applicants applying from abroad for the first time. You're already here, your documentation trail is established, and the process is more straightforward.

When to Apply

Apply no later than 2 months before your current permit expires. We recommend starting 3 months out — SIRI processing times have increased and applying early protects your right to continue working during the renewal period.

Your spouse receives the right to work in Denmark without needing a separate work permit — making it easier for both of you to build your life here.

What You Need to Know

Changing employers always requires a new work permit application submitted to SIRI — your right to work with the new employer only begins once the new permit is approved. We manage the full process so your transition is seamless and compliant.

Timeline

Fast-Track: 15–30 days. Pay Limit: 30–60 days. We'll advise you on which scheme applies and manage your timeline.

Job Seeking Permit
Stay in Denmark legally while looking for work
DKK 2,000
+

What's Included

  • Eligibility assessment for job-seeking permit
  • Gathering and review of all required documents
  • Complete SIRI application preparation & submission on your behalf
  • Coordinating on your behalf any request for further documentation by SIRI
  • Guidance on next steps if no offer is secured within the permit period

Your Situation

If your work permit is linked to an employer and you want to stay in Denmark to look for a new role, a job-seeking permit gives you up to 6 months to do so legally — without losing your right to remain.

Timeline

Apply before your current permit expires. We'll assess your eligibility and submit as quickly as possible.

Did You Know?

If your employer terminated your employment, you are automatically eligible for a 6-month job-seeking permit — even if your original permit hasn't expired yet. You don't need to scramble. You have time, and we'll make sure you use it wisely.

We Understand

Losing your job as an expat is terrifying — your permit, your home, your life in Denmark all feel at risk. We'll give you clarity, options, and a clear action plan. You're not alone in this.

Time Is Critical

You typically have 30–60 days to find new employment before your permit expires. Contact us immediately — the sooner we assess your situation, the more options you have.

Your Next Step

For most people, the immediate next step after losing their job is a Job Seeking Permit — giving you up to 6 months to find new employment legally in Denmark. We'll assess your eligibility and handle the application as part of your consultation.
