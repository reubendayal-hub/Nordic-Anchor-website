Nordic Anchor is committed to protecting your personal data. This policy explains what we collect, why, how long we keep it, and what your rights are under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

2. What Data We Collect and Why

We collect personal data only where there is a clear and legitimate purpose. The table below summarises each category:

Data Purpose Legal Basis Retention
Name, email address, phone number Responding to enquiries submitted via our contact form Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) 24 months from last contact, then deleted
Enquiry content (your message) Understanding your immigration situation to provide relevant advice Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) 24 months from last contact, then deleted
Payment information (when Stripe is enabled) Processing upfront service payments securely Contractual necessity (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) 5 years (Danish bookkeeping requirements)
IP address, browser type, pages visited Website analytics — understanding how visitors use the site Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) — only if you accept cookies Until consent is withdrawn or 13 months

We do not collect any special category data (such as health information, national origin, or immigration status beyond what you voluntarily share in an enquiry message).

3. Contact Forms — Formspree

Our contact form is powered by Formspree, Inc., a US-based third-party service. When you submit a form, your name, email address and message are transmitted to and stored on Formspree's servers.

  • Formspree acts as a data processor on our behalf under a data processing agreement.
  • Data transfers to the US are conducted under standard contractual clauses (SCCs) as per Formspree's privacy framework.
  • You can review Formspree's privacy policy at formspree.io/legal/privacy-policy.

4. Google Fonts

This website loads the Newsreader typeface from Google Fonts (Google LLC, USA). When your browser requests the font files, your IP address is transmitted to Google's servers. This is a technically necessary step to render the site correctly.

  • Google may use this IP address in accordance with their own privacy policy.
  • The legal basis for this transfer is our legitimate interest in providing a correctly rendered website (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).
  • Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy

5. Cookies

We use cookies to make the website function and, with your consent, to understand how it is used. The table below describes the cookies we set:

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
na_cookie_consent Necessary Records whether you have accepted or declined cookies, so we do not show the banner on every visit 12 months
Analytics cookies (if consent given) Analytics Aggregate, anonymised data on page visits and navigation — used to improve the site 13 months

You can withdraw your cookie consent at any time by clicking the link in the footer or by clearing your browser cookies. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before withdrawal.

6. Payment Processing — Stripe (Upcoming)

We intend to offer online payment for services via Stripe, Inc.. When this feature is enabled:

  • Payment card details are entered directly into Stripe's secure, PCI-DSS-compliant payment interface. Nordic Anchor never sees or stores your full card number.
  • Stripe acts as an independent data controller for payment data. Their privacy policy is available at stripe.com/privacy.
  • We retain transaction references (not card data) for 5 years to meet Danish bookkeeping obligations under Bogføringsloven.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal data. We only share data with the third-party processors listed in this policy (Formspree, Google, Stripe) and only to the extent necessary to provide our services. We do not share data with immigration authorities, government bodies or any other third parties without your explicit consent or a clear legal obligation to do so.

8. International Transfers

Some of our processors (Formspree, Google, Stripe) are based in the United States. Transfers to these processors are made under appropriate safeguards — specifically, the EU Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission — ensuring your data receives an equivalent level of protection to that required within the EU/EEA.

9. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you may ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — you may ask us to delete your data where there is no overriding legal basis to retain it.
  • Right to restriction — you may ask us to restrict processing while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to data portability — you may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — you may object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at rd@nordicanchor.dk. We will respond within 30 days. There is no charge for reasonable requests.
